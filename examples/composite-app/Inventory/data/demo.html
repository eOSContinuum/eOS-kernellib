Two principals. Your passkey identity is the human controller; step 8 mints agents it controls. The page holds one session at a time; the banner above always says which principal is acting, and buttons that need a different session disable themselves and say why.
Two planes. This page is the application plane; grants and delegable flags belong to the operator console. The demo deploy pre-provisions the only operator actions the walk needs (a delegable demo capability, granted to your controller at registration); step 7 keeps one refusal alive on purpose so you see the boundary.
The walk: 1 register → 4 create → 6 audit → 7 wipe (403) → 8 mint → 9 list → 12 delegate → 14 agent login → 4 create as the agent → 2 switch back → 10/11 suspend & resume → 15 recovery codes → 16 recover. Streams are live the whole time; the heartbeat line in the log below is the server pushing over SSE every 10 seconds.

Session (your passkey, the human controller)

1 creates the controller identity and logs it in (the demo grants it example:delegation-demo at registration); 2 logs in with the existing passkey and is also how you switch back from an agent session; 3 revokes the current session, whoever it belongs to.

Items and audit (any session; reads are public)

4 mutates as WHOEVER is logged in -- watch the audit trail and the live stream attribute it to the controller or the agent. 5 and 6 are public reads, no session needed. 7 is gated by example:inventory-admin, which nobody in this demo holds: expect 403 from the capability check -- in production an operator grants it on the console (identity grant <uuid> example:inventory-admin).

Agents and delegation (controller session only)

8 mints an agent principal -- the response is the only time the token plaintext exists, and the page fills it into step 14's field. 9 lists your agents with suspension state and delegated capabilities. 10/11 suspend and resume the agent named in the uuid field (suspend also revokes its live sessions). 12 delegates the capability in the field to that agent: example:delegation-demo succeeds because the demo deploy granted it to your controller and flagged it delegable -- try example:inventory-admin to see the refusal an unheld, non-delegable capability gets. 13 withdraws the delegation. Watch step 9 or the live agent stream reflect each change.

Recovery (human flow only)

15 provisions recovery codes for the CONTROLLER while its session is live -- the response is the only time the plaintext exists; store them with the uuid the page fills in. 16 recovers after a lost passkey: it runs a fresh registration ceremony and atomically binds the new passkey to the SAME identity, spending the code. Both are the human flow: 15 disables under an agent session, 16 always re-binds the CONTROLLER uuid, and passkey login (2) is the way back.
